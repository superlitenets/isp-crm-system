INVENTORY MANAGEMENT WORK PLAN
1. Establish Inventory Structure
a. Categorize Items

Network Hardware: ONTs, routers, switches, OLTs, SFPs

CPE Devices: Home routers, WiFi extenders, mesh units

Fiber Materials: Drop cables, ODFs, splitters, pole clamps

Tools & Consumables: Splicing tools, patch cords, RJ45, ducting

Spare Parts: Power adapters, batteries, UPS units

b. Warehouse Layout

Main warehouse

Regional depots

Technician vehicle stock

Reserved stock (projects)

2. Inventory Intake Procedure (Receiving Stock)
a. Receive & Verify

Match against purchase order

Inspect quantities and condition

Log serial numbers where applicable

b. System Entry

Record items in inventory system

Assign item category, storage location, and status

Apply barcode/QR-code labels

c. Store Items

Place items in appropriate bins

Update shelf/bin numbers in the system

3. Stock Disbursement Procedure (Issuing Stock)
a. Stock Request

Triggered by:

New installation

Repair job

Mass rollout (estate/fiber area)

Technician restocking

b. Approval

Supervisor or system auto-approval if within limits.

c. Picking & Handover

Warehouse staff pick stock

Scan items out to technician/vehicle

Technician signs digitally

d. Field Usage Tracking

Items used must be logged per:

Customer account

Ticket or work order

Job type (install/repair)

e. Returns & Adjustments

Unused hardware returned

Faulty items categorized as RMA

Lost items flagged and investigated

4. Inventory Monitoring & Control
a. Daily Tasks

Check stock levels

Process requests

Update returns

b. Weekly Tasks

Reconcile warehouse vs system stock

Update minimum stock thresholds

c. Monthly Tasks

Full stock audit

Report: usage trends, shrinkage, reorder needs

5. Inventory Reporting

Stock on hand (per category, depot, technician)

Stock movement (in/out)

Slow moving items

High demand items

Cost per installation or repair

Expired warranties

Technicians with abnormal stock usage
