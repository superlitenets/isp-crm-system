An Internet Service Provider (ISP) manages inventory and technician accessories effectively by combining clear processes, proper categorization, tracking systems, and accountability. Below is a practical, ISP-focused explanation based on how real networks operate in the field.

1. Types of Inventory an ISP Manages
A. Network & Core Equipment (Warehouse / NOC)

These are high-value, controlled items:

Routers (MikroTik CCR, Huawei, Cisco)

Switches

OLTs / ONTs

Servers

SFPs (1G, 10G, 25G)

Power supplies

Rack accessories

Stored in central warehouse

B. Field Installation Equipment (Technician Tools)

Reusable items issued to technicians:

Fusion splicers

OTDRs

Power meters

Crimping tools

Ladders

Safety gear (helmets, belts)

üìå Management approach

Assigned to a technician (not consumable)

Sign-in / sign-out tracking

Periodic inspection & servicing logs

Loss/damage accountability

C. Consumables & Accessories (High-Volume Items)

Used daily by technicians:

Fiber patch cords

Drop cables

RJ45 connectors

SC/APC & LC connectors

Wall sockets

Cable clips

Screws, ties, conduits

üìå Management approach

Stock levels monitored

Issued per job or per technician

Reorder threshold alerts

Bulk issuance tracked monthly

3. Technician Inventory Management (Critical Part)
A. Technician Kit Assignment

Each technician gets a standard kit:

Defined list of tools

Assigned serial-tracked equipment

B. Job-Based Issuance

Before a job:

Job card created

Required materials auto-suggested

Technician issues items against job

After job:

Used quantities confirmed

Remaining items returned or carried forward

Installed assets linked to customer
